Terms & Definitions

Term Definition
Time 1 month In City = 1 Minute IRL, 1 Year In City = 1 Day IRL (12 months DO NOT EQUAL 1 year). 300 Month Max Sentence
Class 1 Substances Marijuana (Legal up to 10 Grams), Codeine
Class 2 Substances Meth, LSD, XTC, and all other drugs
Prescription An Rx document issued by a registered Doctor to a civilian authorizing the medical use of up to 10 units of an otherwise-restricted Class 1 substance for medicinal purposes.
Public Servant A person who is nominated, appointed, or elected to a position of public office, such as a Judge, a District Attorney or Assistant District Attorney, a Peace Officer, a Medical professional and other similar positions whose primary function is service to the public.
Peace Officer Any person who works for any police department (SASP, BCSO, LSPD, FIB, or a US Marshal)
Conspiracy Agreement with one or more people to commit a crime, with one or more people committing that the act in furtherance of that agreement
Extortion The unlawful exaction of money or property through the threat (or actual) intimidation or violence
Class 1 Weapon Single fired weapons (Non-Automatic) with or without attachments
Class 2 Weapon Highly powered single fired weapons (Non-Automatic) with or without attachments
Class 3 Weapon Automatically fired weapons, Shotguns, Sniper Rifles, or Explosives - with or without attachments
Government Property Any property issued to a public servant by the government, or any facility owned by the Government (Hospital, PD, Courthouse etc.)
Racketeering The act of acquiring a business through illegal activity, operating a business with illegally-derived income, or using a business to commit illegal acts.
Aiding & Abetting Anyone who helps in the commission of a crime shall be charged with the appropriate violations as everyone else participating in the commission of the crime.
Speed Limits Speed limits are 80MPH on Highways, and 50MPH in all other areas.
Court Hold the criminal defendant until trial; defendants lawyer may arrange a bail bond via the District Attorney's office.
Not Guilty Plea Entering a Not Guilty plea in the cells means that the defendant DOES NOT accept the charges and sentencing. Entering this plea means that both parties must attempt to contact a judge for summary judgment or accept preliminary charges until a Court date can be scheduled.
Lawyer Calls Suspects who are being charged with greater than 200 months shall have a lawyer requested for their representation by peace officers.

Specialty Clauses

Clause Description
Heinous Clause Extension of jail time for person(s) who commit serial, violent crimes that are extreme in nature or ones that undermine public trust (EMS/PD/DOJ). Any crimes used under this clause are subject to Judge's Discretion for time / fine.
Repeat Offenders Clause People who are convicted of the same crime three or more times (three incidents) in 30 days are subject to 150% time and fine on their 4th (and subsequent) offences.
Menace Clause People who collect more than 15 felony charges on their record are deemed to consistently cause an undue threat of harm to the public good and may have a warrant issued and be independently tried for lifetime rehabilitative soliatary incarceration
Endangered Fish Clause Sharks and Dolphins are endangered species and must be released if caught.
Endangered Animals Clause Mountain Lions, Pumas, and Cougars are endangered species and must not be shot. If shot accidentally, call 911 and notify the authorities.
